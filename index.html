The four seasons

the year in words.

hopeful spring

New colour and the scent of vegitation, of grass and of blossom fills the air. It's warmer, the outside more inviting. Everthing alive and new!

...this is spring!

new summer

The arroma of spring has gone. Dawn breaks early. The days are longer and brighter, the evenings slower. The air a constant blanket; More heatwaves.

...global warming we're told!

peaceful autumn

It's that time of year when leaves are red and brown and gold and the trees are more bare and the youthful vibrant attire of summer is fading.

...Yet there is a beauty in the bareness!

silent winter

The landscape, White and stark and barren or so the poems used to say. The warm indoors; less often a necessary refrain to the bitting cold!

...again global warming!
